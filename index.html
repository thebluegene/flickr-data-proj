For 8 years now I've shared my photography on Flickr, and after recently gaining an interest in data visualization, I realized posting photos to Flickr has been creating a database of all my photo data. It made perfect sense to use that data of over 1500 photos for my first D3 project. There were four questions I wanted to answer with the visualizations:

  • How apparent is the difference in my photography style during my four attempts at a 365 project?
  • What are my most used settings?
  • When do I take the most photos?

I break up my photography growth into four eras of increased output -- each era coinciding with an attempt at a 365 photo-a-day project. I used a vibrant color npm package, get-image-colors, to assign each photo with a dominant color, because I was curious if I could see any discenerable difference in the colors of my photostream. There wasn't really. Though it makes for a colorful visualisation (this one done with jQuery).
